## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 FAMILY MODE GUIDE

> Skip this section entirely for SOLO mode. Activate for SPOUSE or FAMILY mode.

### Spouse Options — What She/He Can Do in Canada

| Option | Eligibility | Work Rights |
|---|---|---|
| **Spouse Open Work Permit (SOWP)** | Spouse of a Skilled worker (NOC 0/A/B trades) | ✅ Can work any employer in Canada |
| **Visitor Visa (TRV)** | Any spouse — no work authorization | ❌ Cannot work |
| **Study Permit** | If spouse enrolled in a DLI | ✅ Part-time work (20 hrs/wk) |

> **Key rule:** SOWP eligibility depends on the principal applicant's NOC level.
> - NOC TEER 2 & 3 trades (electrician 7241, plumber 7251, welder 7237, truck driver 7511, etc.) → **Spouse IS eligible for SOWP**
> - NOC TEER 4 & 5 trades (general labourer 7611, farm worker 8431, etc.) → **Spouse gets Visitor Visa only** (no open work permit)

### Spouse Document Checklist (SOWP or Visitor Visa)

- [ ] Valid passport (6+ months beyond intended stay)
- [ ] Pakistani CNIC (both sides, attested)
- [ ] Nikah Nama (marriage certificate — NADRA attested + translated to English if in Urdu)
- [ ] 2 passport-size photos (IRCC spec)
- [ ] Proof of relationship (wedding photos, joint bank account, utility bills)
- [ ] Proof of principal applicant's work permit approval
- [ ] Spouse's own bank statement (or joint) — min CAD 2,000–3,000 recommended
- [ ] Medical exam (Panel Physician — if staying 6+ months)
- [ ] Police Clearance (NADRA) for spouse
- [ ] Biometrics at VFS (if first-time Canada visa)
- [ ] IMM 5710 — Application for Open Work Permit (if applying SOWP)

### Dependent Children Checklist (Study Permit or Visitor)

- [ ] Passport for each child
- [ ] Birth certificate (NADRA attested + English translation)
- [ ] CNIC / B-Form for each child
- [ ] Proof of enrollment (if study permit — school/DLI acceptance letter)
- [ ] Medical exam per child (if staying 6+ months)
- [ ] Police Clearance not required for minors under 18
- [ ] Children under 18 travel on parent's visitor or study permit (no separate work permit)

### Family Timing Strategy

| Scenario | Recommended Approach |
|---|---|
| Client + spouse fly together | Apply for work permit + SOWP simultaneously — same VFS appointment |
| Spouse follows within 3 months | Client departs first, sponsor spouse from Canada once settled |
| Children in school in Pakistan | Spouse visits first; children join after school year ends or on study permit |
| Full family departs together | All applications submitted same day — biometrics same VFS slot |

## OCCUPATION PROFILES & NOC CODES

> NOC code is required on ALL LMIA and work permit documents. Never proceed without it.

### 🚛 Truck Driver
- **NOC:** 7511 (Transport Truck Driver)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta, Saskatchewan, BC, Manitoba, Ontario
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round; grain transport Aug–Nov (SK)
- **License required:** Pakistani Class 1 / HTV / PSV commercial license
- **Key certifications:** Air brake endorsement, Hazmat, logbook training
- **Min experience:** 2+ years preferred by Canadian employers

### ⚡ Electrician
- **NOC:** 7241 (Electrician, Except Industrial & Power System) / 7242 (Industrial Electrician)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta, Ontario, BC, Nova Scotia
- **Seasonal peak:** Construction season April–October
- **License required:** Pakistani electrical trade certificate / diploma
- **Key certifications:** Safety orientation, WHMIS, First Aid
- **Note:** Canadian Red Seal not required for LMIA entry but helps PR pathway

### 🔧 Plumber
- **NOC:** 7251 (Plumber)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta, Ontario, BC
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round (construction & infrastructure)
- **License required:** Pakistani plumbing trade certificate
- **Key certifications:** Gas fitting (if applicable), WHMIS, confined space
- **Note:** High shortage in AB & ON — strong LMIA approval rate

### 🔩 Pipefitter / Steamfitter
- **NOC:** 7252 (Steamfitter-Pipefitter)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta (oil sands), BC, Ontario
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round; oil patch turnarounds Mar & Sep
- **License required:** Trade certificate, welding experience an asset
- **Key certifications:** Pressure fitting, rigging basics, WHMIS

### 🔥 Welder
- **NOC:** 7237 (Welder & Machine Operator)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta, Ontario, Saskatchewan, Nova Scotia
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round; oil & gas, shipbuilding, manufacturing
- **License required:** Trade certificate + welding procedure qualifications (SMAW, MIG, TIG, FCAW)
- **Key certifications:** CWB (Canadian Welding Bureau) — not required to enter but boosts chances

### 🪚 Carpenter
- **NOC:** 7271 (Carpenter)
- **Demand provinces:** BC, Ontario, Alberta
- **Seasonal peak:** Construction season Apr–Oct
- **License required:** Trade certificate / apprenticeship records
- **Key certifications:** Framing, formwork, finishing experience

### 🏗️ General Construction Labourer
- **NOC:** 7611 (Construction Trades Helper & Labourer)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta, BC, Ontario, Nova Scotia
- **Seasonal peak:** April–October (construction season)
- **License required:** None required — experience letter sufficient
- **Key certifications:** WHMIS, Fall Arrest, First Aid preferred
- **Note:** Easiest NOC to qualify for — high volume of LMIA approvals

### 🏚️ Roofer
- **NOC:** 7291 (Roofer & Shingles Applicator)
- **Demand provinces:** BC, Ontario, Alberta
- **Seasonal peak:** May–October
- **License required:** Experience letter / trade record
- **Key certifications:** Fall Arrest (mandatory for Canadian sites), WHMIS

### 🧱 Mason / Bricklayer
- **NOC:** 7281 (Bricklayer)
- **Demand provinces:** Ontario, BC, Alberta, Quebec
- **Seasonal peak:** Construction season
- **License required:** Trade certificate or experience letter
- **Key certifications:** Scaffold safety, WHMIS

### 🏗️ Heavy Equipment Operator
- **NOC:** 7521 (Heavy Equipment Operator, except Crane)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta (oil sands), BC (mining), Saskatchewan
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round in AB/SK; construction Apr–Oct elsewhere
- **License required:** Pakistani HTV / equipment operator certificate
- **Key certifications:** Dozer, excavator, grader, loader operation experience

### ❄️ HVAC Technician
- **NOC:** 7313 (Heating, Refrigeration & Air Conditioning Mechanic)
- **Demand provinces:** Ontario, BC, Alberta
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round (installation & service)
- **License required:** HVAC/refrigeration trade certificate
- **Key certifications:** Refrigerant handling, electrical basics

### 🎨 Painter & Decorator
- **NOC:** 7294 (Painter & Decorator, except Interior Decorator)
- **Demand provinces:** BC, Ontario, Alberta
- **Seasonal peak:** April–October
- **License required:** Experience letter / trade record
- **Key certifications:** Fall Arrest, spray painting experience, surface preparation

### 🪨 Concrete Finisher
- **NOC:** 7282 (Concrete Finisher)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta, Ontario, BC
- **Seasonal peak:** Construction season
- **License required:** Experience letter
- **Key certifications:** WHMIS, pump operation

### 🌾 Farm / Agricultural Worker
- **NOC:** 8431 (Nursery & Greenhouse Worker) / 8432 (Harvesting Labourer)
- **Demand provinces:** BC (Okanagan), Ontario, Saskatchewan, Alberta, PEI
- **Seasonal peak:** May–November (harvest season) ← **highest LMIA volume in Canada**
- **License required:** None — experience letter sufficient
- **Key certifications:** Pesticide handling, tractor operation useful
- **Note:** Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program (SAWP) available for Pakistani workers

### 🏭 Food Processing / Meat Packing Worker
- **NOC:** 9462 (Industrial Butcher & Meat Cutter) / 9617 (Food & Beverage Processing Labourer)
- **Demand provinces:** Alberta (meat packing), Ontario, BC
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round
- **License required:** None — experience letter sufficient
- **Key certifications:** Food safety, WHMIS, forklift (asset)

### 📦 Warehouse / Forklift Operator
- **NOC:** 7452 (Material Handler) / 7521 (if heavy equipment)
- **Demand provinces:** Ontario, BC, Alberta, Quebec
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round; peaks Nov–Jan (holiday logistics)
- **License required:** Forklift certificate (obtain in Pakistan if possible)
- **Key certifications:** WHMIS, forklift license, inventory management

### 🛡️ Security Guard
- **NOC:** 6541 (Security Guard & Related Security Service Occupations)
- **Demand provinces:** Ontario, BC, Alberta
- **Seasonal peak:** Year-round
- **License required:** No formal trade cert — clean criminal record essential
- **Key certifications:** First Aid, CPR, Security Guard license (obtained in Canada after arrival)

### 🔗 Ironworker / Structural Steel Worker
- **NOC:** 7236 (Ironworker)
- **Demand provinces:** Ontario, BC, Alberta
- **Seasonal peak:** Construction season
- **License required:** Trade certificate / apprenticeship record
- **Key certifications:** Rigging, crane signal, fall arrest

## PHASE 4 — LMIA PROCESS

Once a job offer is received, initiate LMIA coordination immediately.

### LMIA Basics
- **Applicant:** Canadian **employer** applies to ESDC
- **Fee:** CAD 1,000 per position (employer pays — confirm this before proceeding)
- **Processing time:** 2–10 weeks depending on stream and province
- **Result:** Positive LMIA letter → client applies for Work Permit

### LMIA Document Checklist — Employer Side
- [ ] CRA Business Number + business registration
- [ ] Proof of recruitment efforts (Canadian ads placed before hiring TFW)
- [ ] Signed employment contract (wage, hours, province, job duties)
- [ ] Proof of financial ability to pay offered wage
- [ ] Job description matching the NOC code exactly

### LMIA Document Checklist — Client Side
- [ ] Valid passport (6+ months beyond intended stay)
- [ ] Trade certificate / license (certified copy)
- [ ] Resume (Canadian format — built in Phase 1)
- [ ] Job offer / employment contract
- [ ] Passport-size photos (IRCC spec)

9. **Follow-up is mandatory** — Day 5 email, Day 10 phone/LinkedIn, Day 14 escalation.
10. **All Tier 1 agencies contacted in Week 1** — no exceptions.
11. **LMIA belongs to the employer** — guide employer but ensure client docs are ready the moment LMIA is approved.

16. **Start PR planning from Day 1 in Canada** — tell clients their PR pathway immediately.
