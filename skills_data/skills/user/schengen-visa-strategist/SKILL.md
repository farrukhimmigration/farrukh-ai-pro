## Phase 3: High-Vigilance Factor Analysis

Scrutinize every of these "Ties to Pakistan" indicators with extreme care:

### Economic Ties
- Employment letter: permanent vs. contractual, salary consistency, leave approval
- Self-employed: Chamber of Commerce registration, business registration, 2-year FBR returns
- Suspicious: Sudden salary increase, unverifiable employer, informal sector income

### Financial Ties
- **Personal bank statement** (6-month): average balance, transaction pattern, large/sudden deposits — preferred by most consulates
- **Employer/Company financials**: acceptable at many consulates if personal statement is absent or thin — must include employer bank statement, salary certificate, and sponsorship/guarantee letter
- If client has **no personal bank statement**, build the financial case entirely around employer documents; note this clearly in cover letter
- Liquid assets vs. trip cost: minimum **€100/day × trip duration** as a safety threshold (whether from personal or employer-evidenced funds)
- 🚩 RED FLAG: Round-number deposits, single large top-up just before statement, circular transactions
- 🚩 RED FLAG: Submitting employer financials without a proper employer sponsorship letter — always pair them together

### Family & Social Ties
- NADRA FRC (Family Registration Certificate)
- Nikah Nama if married
- Minor children or elderly parents in Pakistan (strong retention indicator)
- 🚩 RED FLAG: All immediate family already abroad, no dependents in Pakistan

### Emotional / Intent to Return
- Prior visas (especially Schengen/UK/US) with clean return records
- Community ties: property, long-standing employment, business ownership
- 🚩 RED FLAG: Applied multiple times and been rejected, no reason given to return
