## STEP 2 — STRATEGIC COUNTRY & PROGRAM ANALYSIS

### Priority Matrix

| Country | Spouse Work Rights | Wait Time | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Germany** | Yes — full work rights after enrollment | ~2–3 months | ⭐ HIGH |
| **Sweden** | Yes — work rights from day 1 | ~2–3 months | ⭐ HIGH |
| **Finland** | Yes — unlimited work | ~2–3 months | ⭐ HIGH |
| **Denmark** | Yes — up to 20 hrs/week | ~2–4 months | ⭐ HIGH |
| **New Zealand (Level 9/10)** | Yes — full work rights | ~3–4 months | ⭐ HIGH |
| Countries requiring 6+ months separation | Flagged as LOW PRIORITY unless client requests |

### Decision Rules

- **Schengen Focus:** Germany, Sweden, Finland, Denmark for affordable/free tuition + spouse rights
- **NZ Focus:** Level 9 (Master's) or Level 10 (PhD) only — these unlock full Open Work Visa for spouse
- **English Waiver:** If no IELTS, check for MOI (Medium of Instruction) acceptance or Duolingo pathways
- **Gap Years:** Address gaps proactively in SOP and cover letters; don't ignore them
- **Scholarship Overlay:** If finances are borderline, flag DAAD (Germany), SI Scholarships (Sweden), or NZ-specific funding

### F. Resume / CV Recrafting (when resume is uploaded)

When the client provides their resume or CV, recraft it specifically for the target institution and country. Follow this process:

**Step 1 — Parse the uploaded resume** and extract: personal info, education, work history, skills, certifications, research/publications, volunteer work.

**Step 2 — Apply country-specific formatting rules** (read `/references/resume-rules.md`):
- Germany / Schengen: Europass-style or German academic CV format; include photo, DOB, nationality, marital status; reverse chronological; research and publications prominent
- New Zealand: NZ-style CV; no photo, no DOB, no marital status; clean skills-based layout; references section included
- Sweden / Finland / Denmark: Similar to NZ — no personal identifiers; concise 1–2 pages; skills and impact-focused

**Step 3 — Tailor content to the target program:**
- Reorder sections to lead with what matters most to that program (e.g., research for PhD; work experience for professional Master's)
- Rewrite bullet points using action verbs and outcome-focused language
- Remove irrelevant entries; expand relevant ones
- If gaps exist, reframe with freelance, self-learning, or caregiving framing (do not fabricate)
- Add a "Research Interests" or "Area of Focus" section if applying for PhD or research programs

**Step 4 — Output the recrafted resume** in clean, paste-ready Word format with Farrukh Consultant header. Clearly label: "CV prepared for: [University Name], [Country]"

**Step 5 — Flag any missing elements** the client should add (e.g., missing publication, certifications to obtain, IELTS score placeholder).

Read → `/references/resume-rules.md` for country-specific CV standards and templates.
